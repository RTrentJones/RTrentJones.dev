I'm Trent. I'm a senior software engineer at Amazon, where I build the developer platforms and
release tooling that make other engineers faster. Before this I was the second engineer at [Fulgent Genetics](https://www.fulgentgenetics.com/),
building the variant-curation platform clinical scientists used to call genetic variants. I studied
neuroscience and computer science at USC, and the biology still pulls on what I build.

Most of my work is invisible from the outside: backend services, release pipelines, and
infrastructure other teams rely on. This site is the exception. It's a collection of self-verifying
systems.

There's one rule for everything hosted here: it has to prove it works, continuously, without me in
the loop. Not "it worked when I wrote the README." If a link is marked live on this site, it's
actively checked by the same harness that deployed it — and if it ever stops working, that harness is
meant to catch it before I do.

Agentic development is what makes that reach possible. It lets me take an idea past the prototype and
all the way to something that runs in production, verifies itself, and stays up — the unglamorous last
mile that used to be where personal projects died. The work here isn't smaller for it; it's more
finished.

What to expect from the blog: technical write-ups on the systems themselves — platform and
verification work, protocol-level engineering, and the places where software meets the life sciences.
The kind of thing I'd want to read from someone else.

More as I build it.
